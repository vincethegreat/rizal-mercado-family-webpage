Rizal's Arrest

From July 6 to 14 he was in a state of incommunicado. The same night that that Rizal was held incommunicado at Fort Santiago, a group of resolute men secretly met in the house of Deodato Arellano. Arellano, Andres Bonifacio, Valentin Diaz, Teodoro Plata, Jose Dizon and Ladislao Diwa It has been said that it was at this meeting that Katipunan was founded.

Rizal was placed under arrest and escorted to Fort Santiago by Ramon Despujol, nephew and aide of Governor General Despujol. July 7, 1892 Gaceta de Manila published the story of Rizal's arrest which produced commotion Filipino indignant among people, particularly the members of the newly organized Liga Filipina July 7, 1892 the same issue of Gaceta Governor Despujol's contained General decree deporting Rizal to "one of the islands in the South"

Rizal was brought under heavy| guard to the steamer Cebu, which was sailing for Dapitan The steamer was under Captain Delgras departed at 1:00 AM, July 15, sailing south, passing Mindoro and panay and reaching Dapitan on Sunday, the 17th of July at 7:00 in the evening

Rizal began his exile in lonely Dapitan, a period of four years
